14. Based strictly on the filings, contrast the core business models of Caterpillar and Coca-Cola.
- Based strictly on the provided filings, the core business models of Caterpillar and Coca-Cola contrast as follows:

Caterpillar (CAT) operates through two reportable segments: Machinery, Power & Energy and Financial Products.

The Machinery, Power & Energy segment is primarily engaged in the design, manufacturing and marketing of heavy equipment and power systems (Source 3).
The Financial Products segment provides financing and insurance to customers and dealers for the purchase and lease of Caterpillar and other equipment (Source 3).
The company sells most products through a global dealer network, with some direct sales of turbines and locomotives (Source 4). Its business model thus combines industrial manufacturing with integrated financing.
Coca-Cola (KO) is a total beverage company whose business is primarily nonalcoholic beverages (Source 5). The company generates revenue from two types of operations (Source 2):

Concentrate operations – manufacturing and selling beverage concentrates and syrups to bottling partners. These operations account for about 59% of net revenues and yield higher gross profit margins but lower absolute revenue per unit.
Finished product operations – manufacturing and selling finished beverages (through consolidated bottling operations). These account for about 41% of net revenues and yield lower gross profit margins but higher absolute revenues.
The company’s segment structure includes geographic operating segments (EMEA, Latin America, North America, Asia Pacific) and a Bottling Investments segment for its consolidated bottlers (Source 2). The core business model relies on a brand‑licensing, concentrate‑based system complemented by direct bottling.
Key Contrast:

Caterpillar’s model is industrial manufacturing + captive financial services to support equipment sales.
Coca-Cola’s model is branded beverage concentrates + finished product bottling, with concentrate sales being the higher‑margin core.
